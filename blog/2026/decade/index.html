Last month, the Seattle Seahawks became World Champions — the Super Bowl 60 parade rolled through downtown Seattle. That moment also quietly marked 10 years since I first called this city home.

Today, March 14, 2026, is AWS's 20th birthday. It is also my 10-year anniversary at Amazon.

It is a genuine privilege to have been part of the first half of those 20 years — to build, to ship, and to grow alongside this company from the inside.

I joined on March 14, 2016 — the same calendar date as AWS's founding, March 14, 2006. Today marks 3,652 Day 1s. I joined when Jeff Bezos was still CEO. His words have stayed with me ever since: "Customer obsession, and focus on what does not change." It is almost poetic — a company named after a river.

My work has been featured in 4 earnings reports and publicly recognized by CEOs. While work spans different products and services, the underlying theme has always been the same: customers.

A Note on the Industry

There have been thoughtful conversations lately about where technologists should plant their flag in the LLM era — including "The Cost of Staying" and Anthropic's Labor Market Impacts research. I agree that research labs and AI startups have emerged as a powerful new center of gravity. At the same time, I still see profound opportunities within large technology companies — to innovate at real scale, with real customers and real constraints. The clock is ticking. Continuing to tackle hard problems and build new skills is the only answer.

What Does Not Change

  • Keep doing hard and impactful work — that never changes.
  • Stay humble and keep learning.
  • Deep gratitude to every leader and manager who believed in me — none of this happens without your support.
